This uses interactive slideshow to highlight effect of COVID-19 on US mobility. The mobility data is based on location data as reported by mobile devices. The data is anonymized and cannot be used to identify any individual. The mobility is defined by a "mobility index" that indicates the change in mobility from the "normal" mobility before the onset of the virus. This is plotted on the Y axis of all the charts. This allows to better compare and identify the impact of the virus in different US states. The X axis of the charts has consists of a timeline. The following three charts can be seen by choosing the appropriate radio buttons:
An overview that shows the overall mobility index for US for the last few months, and along with that the overall number of cases.
A state-level mobility data and co-relation with number of COVID cases in the state.
Finally, a combined view of the mobility data for the 5 states that have the highest number of COVID cases.
Circles on some of the charts provide tooltips that show additional information on hover.
